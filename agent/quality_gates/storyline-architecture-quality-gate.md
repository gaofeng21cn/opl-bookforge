# Storyline Architecture Quality Gate

Quality gate declaration is required for every generated OPL-compatible stage.
Dedicated review stage is conditional: create one when storyline decisions change owner intent, chapter ordering, evidence scope, publication promise, or editorial position.

Pass conditions:

- The premise, audience promise, chapter thesis chain, evidence map, and style contract are explicit refs.
- Each chapter has a role in the book-level arc and a clear reason to exist.
- Source gaps, unsupported claims, owner decisions, and sequencing risks are listed as direct evidence refs or typed blockers.
- The proposed voice uses affirmative, specific, human prose and avoids generic AI-sounding transitions.
- The next stage receives stable structure for chapter drafting, illustration/table planning, and layout decisions.

Fail-closed conditions:

- Missing source corpus, audience, owner intent, publication form, or style ref.
- Chapter plan is a topic list without argument movement.
- Review relies on shared execution context instead of direct evidence refs.
- Any generated surface tries to claim final book quality, export readiness, or publication approval.
