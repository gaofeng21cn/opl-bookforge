# Reader Style Contract Skill

Use this skill inside `storyline-architecture` before drafting or expanding chapters. It fixes who the book is for and what prose will feel natural to those readers.

Working policy:

- Identify primary, secondary, and excluded readers from the owner brief, source plan, comparable books, and topic context.
- Treat primary readers as the writing target. Secondary readers may define accessibility constraints only when compatible with the primary reader promise; they must not create separate chapter obligations, explanatory detours, lower-density exposition, difficulty downgrades, or voice targets unless the owner explicitly promotes them to co-primary readers.
- Name the readers' likely reading situation, prior knowledge, anxieties, practical questions, and tolerance for theory, examples, jargon, and persuasion.
- Define natural expression for this specific book: stance, vocabulary, sentence rhythm, paragraph movement, example density, metaphor boundary, emotional temperature, and forbidden voice patterns.
- Record the author/source stance for major cases before drafting: outsider observation, participant-observer, project designer, project executor, evaluator, or advocate. If the owner/source material shows the author team designed, executed, or directly participated in a case, the contract must define how practice-involved voice may appear in the manuscript. Do not silently default that case to neutral third-party public-source observation.
- For practice-involved cases, define the allowed pronouns, stance, evidence boundary, and forbidden voice patterns. The prose should explain design judgment, operational choices, and reflective limits in an active voice while still marking unsupported outcomes, missing interviews, missing owner authorization, missing user feedback, or missing learning-effect evidence as typed evidence gaps.
- Convert the definition into a durable `reader-style-contract` ref that later chapters can cite during drafting, QC, style checks, and revision.
- If the owner did not specify readers or voice and Book Forge cannot infer them with high confidence from the source material, ask the owner before drafting body prose; do not silently choose a generic style.
- When Book Forge makes a high-confidence inference, record the evidence and mark the contract as `inferred_pending_owner_review`.

This skill does not authorize final editorial acceptance. It creates the reader/style target used by later quality gates.
