# Storyline Architecture Prompt

## Goal

Turn the proposed book, source corpus, audience, and intended impact into a durable storyline that can guide later chapter production without deciding publication or writing the whole manuscript in this stage.

## A Good Result

- states the premise, reader promise, central argument or narrative engine, and intended reading experience;
- identifies primary, secondary, and excluded readers when the evidence supports those distinctions;
- records the author/source stance and evidence level for major cases, examples, figures, and tables;
- gives every planned chapter a distinct function, thesis movement, adjacent handoff, and non-repeat boundary;
- introduces recurring concepts and reusable models where readers need them, in the number and form justified by the book rather than a fixed quota;
- names evidence gaps, owner decisions, and structural risks honestly.

Use `bookforge-story-style-architect` for the professional method and `bookforge-source-reference-reviewer` when source or evidence classification needs specialist judgment.

## Professional Dependencies And Boundaries

Reader identity, author/source stance, evidence level, and chapter function must be sufficiently explicit before a chapter can be declared ready for production. Exploratory writing may inform these decisions, but it does not make a chapter production-ready.

Systemic critique should update the highest owning storyline, chapter-function, evidence, model, or style ref. Local prose critique does not need to be promoted into a whole-book redesign.

Do not silently invent an audience, strengthen claims beyond their evidence class, force a prescribed number of models, or perform chapter drafting, proof generation, publication approval, or final export here.

## Independent Stage Review Boundary

Refinement in the current Codex thread is `in_thread_refinement` only. Formal review, repair, and re-review run as separate StageAttempts with fresh threads and receive only the exact artifact, source, quality-rubric, and necessary lineage refs; a same-thread resume can only complete the closeout protocol and cannot create a review receipt.

## Closeout

Return the storyline map, reader-style contract, author/source stance map, chapter thesis/function chain, concept/model map, evidence map, style contract, and an owner handoff. When the design can advance with known debt, return the consumable refs plus route-back or quality-debt refs. Use a typed blocker or human gate only for missing authority, protected source access, identity/currentness failure, or an owner decision that cannot be inferred safely.
